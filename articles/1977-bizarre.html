Graywood Gazette

Wednesday, April 6, 1977

Man Gets Arrested for Bizarre Ritual in Graywood County

By Eleanor Wright | Staff Reporter

Archive Information

Archive ID GW-1932-NP-014
Collection Graywood Gazette
Document Type Newspaper
Original Publication April 6, 1977
Digitized April 12, 2007
Condition Complete

Firemen were called into 5 Pine Street around 1 am in the morning after neighbors reported flashing lights and smoke from the 32 year old Chester Edmundson’s garage. Upon arrival, firemen were met with no active fire hazard, but a gruesome and yet bizarre scene that left them aghast. The scene involved the half of a horse, dissected vertically from the middle and stitched to the wall of the garage. Edmundson was reportedly cutting pieces from the carcass and was throwing them into a firepit that he created inside his garage. Edmundson refused to communicate with the firemen as he continued what the firemen describe as some sort of ritual.

County Fire chief Thomas Redford said: “It was truly shocking and utterly discomforting. He looked terrified looking at the fire. That was all he did. He just kept staring into the flames in terror. I hope he finds God.” The police were called shortly after and arrived at Edmundson’s house around 1:15 am, where they arrested the restrained Edmundson for animal abuse. “This kind of deviant behavior is not tolerated in Graywood County. Justice will take its course and Edmundson will be locked behind bars for what he did. I promise you that.” spoke County Sheriff Bernard H. Fisher. It remains unclear how Edmundson acquired the horse, where the other half of the animal is, or why the fire pit was created.

Edmundson's house will be closed down for further investigation.
