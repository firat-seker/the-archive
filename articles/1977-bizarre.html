Man Gets Arrested for Bizarre Ritual in Graywool County April 6, 1977 Firemen were called into 5 Pine Street around 1 am in the morning after neighbors reported flashing lights that looked like fire inside the 32 year old Chester Edmundson’s garage. Upon arrival, firemen were met with no active fires but a gruesome and bizarre scene. The scene involved the half of a horse, dissected vertically from the middle and stitched to the wall of Edmundson’s garage. Edmundson was reportedly cutting pieces from the carcass and was throwing them into a firepit that he created inside his garage, and refused to communicate with the firemen. Captain Thomas Redford of the fire response team spoke: “It was truly shocking, truly. And utterly discomforting. He looked terrified looking at the fire. That was all he did. He just kept staring into the fire in terror. I hope this man finds God.” The police were called shortly after and arrived at Edmundson’s house around 1:15 am, where they arrested Edmundson who had been restrained by the firemen for animal abuse. “This kind of deviant behavior is not tolerated in Graywool County. Justice will take its course and Edmundson will be locked behind bars for what he did, I promise you that,” spoke Deputy Sheriff Gary H. Fisher. It remains unclear how Edmundson acquired the horse, or why the fire pit was created.
